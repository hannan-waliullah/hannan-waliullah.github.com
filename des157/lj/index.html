Response 3: Images and interaction

11 Jan 2021

My project is about the cookies I have baked in the past and the stories behind them, reminiscent of a food blog, but interactive. This photo below below shows one of my cookies: the snickerdoodle. It is one of the four cookies that I have chosen to showcase for my project. While the photo is not anything spectacular, it highlights the character and patterns of the cinnamon-infused cookie. It also has a story behind it, why I make it, and the memories associated with it. These cookies were my sisters favorite, and we’d make them together. When my sister moved to college I would mail them from my town to her college in order to stay connected with her.

Amanda’s project is about her parents and their history. The photos have such a rich character. They feel like older polaroids, and imply stories. All three photos include two people: her parents, and two of the photos also include other people. These are truly an example of how a picture can be a thousand words. While the images are of Amanda's family smiling in the photo (like how family photos are usually taken), there seems more to each image than meets the eye. It leaves me wondering : what is the story behind each image? Why were they taken? How has this shaped Amanda to who she is today?

Response 3: Images and interaction

21 Jan 2021

The pudding is a news-website which focuses on multimedia journalism. Their publication explores different forms of image manipulation, data visualization, and other interactive techniques for journalistic storytelling. The story “Best Year in Music”, takes the reader through a journey of all the number 1 singles from the 1960’s to modern day.

In this story, the albums-art images are put onto a moving 3-d graph, with the albums “approaching” the screen as time moves forward. Additionally, the user is able to fast-forwards (or move back), in order to select any desired year. Finally, the number one song (at the charts current date) is playing in the background, in order to create a more immersive experience. The page is also able to be paused or muted, making it more accessible.

This page was interesting for me because it used images and interactivity in multiple effective instances. For one, despite the user not being able to click or hover over the image explicitly, the image still had the sense of interactivity through the pause/play button. Another reason is that it gave users freedom to choose a year. Finally, the music being played really highlighted the immersiveness of an audio-visual experience.

Response 2: Modal Design

28 Jan 2021

Modals (also known as overlays), can be a very powerful means of interaction when used properly. In her article “Best practices for Modals/ Overlays/ Dialog windows”, author Naema Baskanderi explains different practices in order to provide the best user experience when using overlays. Modals are elements that sit on top of the applications main window, forcing interaction with the user.

Baskanderi provides different strategies for creating an ideal modal experience. For example, designers should provide an easy way to exit the modal window. In many advertisements today, the “close” button appears a few seconds after the rest of the modal (which in my opinion is not good UX). Additionally, modals should be properly titles, and buttons clearly labelled. We should consider different visual elements when designing a modal-- such as their location & size, and using a lightbox effect surrounding the modal. Finally, we can make modals more accessible through the use of keyboard shortcuts.

When used properly, modals can provide a simpler UI and a more attentive user experience. However, when used improperly, modals can be akin to a pop-up window: the user will probably ignore it.

Response 1: Form Design

21 Jan 2021

When designing a form, designers should strive to make it easy and approachable for the user. In the article “Best Practices for Form Design”, author Salim Ansari lists 20 different considerations when creating an interactive form. It’s important to keep in mind all of the different aspects of the form, from the writing of the input fields to the visual hierarchy of the form. When writing a form, we should eliminate unnecessary questions and gradually increase the complexity of questions. We should avoid using negative language and blaming the user.

Additionally, we have to consider the user interface of the form. This includes placement of the text, alignment, size, color, etc. The author points out smaller things we can design for in order to enhance the user experience. For example, highlighting the first input box can make it easier for the user to locate it. It’s important for the form to be accessible to all users. Accessibility of the form includes proper contrast, text size, and other considerations.
